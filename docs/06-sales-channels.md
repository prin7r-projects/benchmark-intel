## Channel rationale

### Twitter / X (organic)
The audience is concentrated. Eval leads, applied scientists, and AI engineers follow each other and amplify methodology-rich threads. We post:
- Each weekly digest (link + 4-tweet thread).
- "Methodology bumped" footnote on any source that changed scoring rubric.
- "Cross-source check" threads that show where a vendor's claim diverges from the cross-source value.

### HN front page (organic)
We submit selectively. Triggers:
- A vendor blog claim diverges from cross-source. The submission title is a calm, specific delta.
- A new benchmark source comes online and we add it to the methodology table.
- A retrospective on a model release (60-day later analysis).

We do not submit "the leaderboard updated this week".

### Newsletter cross-promo
Latent Space, AI Engineer Pack, Hacker Newsletter, ImportAI. Trade: a pinned blurb in our digest for theirs, or a paid `~$300` placement when traction warrants. Acceptance criteria: the partner audience overlaps with Mira persona by ≥70%.

### Podcast guest spots
Founder appears as guest. Pitch is "we built the cross-source audit so you don't have to run six benchmarks every Monday." Asset: a one-pager with that month's most cited Triad chart.

### Stratechery / SemiAnalysis comp
Comp the analyst tier free for two weeks. The conversion happens when an analyst quotes Triad in a memo and their fund pays for the Custom-slices tier afterwards.

### Direct outbound (Enterprise)
Targets: AI Centre-of-Excellence leads at F500. Lever: a custom slice mockup in the cold-email body. Cadence: 80 contacts/quarter, response rate ~6%, demo rate ~2%, close rate ~30% of demos.

### Conference attendance
Founder + one engineer at NeurIPS (Dec) and ICLR (Apr). Booth-free; bench-time meetings only. Goal: 4 high-quality F500 conversations per conference.

### The digest itself
The digest is the most important channel. Each issue is forwarded internally inside subscriber companies. We instrument the digest with a "forwarded by" link that lets us see the second-degree audience without tracking individual readers.

## Channel anti-patterns

- Paid LinkedIn ads. Conversion is poor at the $29/mo price point and the audience is anti-marketing.
- Sponsored Twitter posts. Hurts brand credibility; eval leads punish sponsorship.
- Listicle SEO content. The audience does not read "top 10 LLMs in 2026" articles. Methodology-rich essays only.
- Sponsoring a benchmark leaderboard. Conflict of interest. We never accept benchmark sponsorship and we publish a no-sponsorship policy on the methodology page.

## Distribution operating principles

- Every distribution piece must include a footnote chip and a methodology link. We do not distribute without sourcing.
- We never publish vendor-comparable graphics with logos. We publish cross-source numbers with footnotes.
- We never write "10×", "game-changing", "next-generation". The brand voice is empirical.
- We treat retention as the marketing KPI; if retention drops, content production stops until the issue is found.
