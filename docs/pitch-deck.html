02 / 10 · The problem

AI engineers maintain spreadsheets to know what's working this week.

  • ~6h/week of senior engineer time spent stitching vendor blogs, lmsys, ArtificialAnalysis, HELM, and SWE-bench into a private comparison sheet.
  • Vendor leaderboards cherry-pick the benchmarks they win on. Single-benchmark sites are siloed. HELM is rigorous but stale.
  • The harness layer — the same model in different scaffolds — is not measured anywhere public. It is now the biggest source of variance in agent performance.

07 / 10 · Methodology and credibility

Zero vendor sponsorship. Every cell footnotes the source row.

  • Funded by subscription only. Published policy. We never accept compensation from a model lab, agent vendor, harness vendor, or benchmark organisation.
  • Cross-source normalisation: canonical row schema for every ingested score. Layer-aware percentile rank published.
  • Methodology versioning: when an upstream changes its scoring rubric, we re-ingest the affected history and surface a "methodology bumped" footnote on every cell for 30 days.
  • We aggregate; we do not currently run our own benchmarks.
